Architecture: The thermistor forms a voltage divider with an internal ECM pull-up resistor tied to 5V. The TAN wire (pin 32) carries both the supply current and the signal voltage — the ECM reads the divided voltage at this node. The GY/R wire (pin 49) is the dedicated sensor return.

Flow direction: Current exits the ECM's internal 5V rail, flows through the pull-up, out pin 32, along TAN to terminal A, through the thermistor, out terminal B, back along GY/R to pin 49, and to sensor ground inside the ECM.

Architecture: Three dedicated wires — LG/R (5V reference, pin 50), LG (signal out, pin 33), and BK/LG (sensor ground, pin 49). The sensor is a potentiometer with a wiper. Supply current flows through the full resistive element from pin 50 to pin 49; the signal wire on pin 33 is a high-impedance voltage tap at the wiper position — the ECM draws almost no current on it.

Flow direction: 5V exits pin 50 → LG/R → terminal C → down through pot element → terminal A → BK/LG → back to pin 49 (sensor ground). The LG signal wire carries only microamps to the ECM's ADC.
